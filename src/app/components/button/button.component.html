Buttons

Buttons express what action will occur when the user clicks or touches it. Buttons are used to initialize an action, either in the background or foreground of an experience.

There are different styles and sizes for buttons and it's important to use the correct button type to communicate its place in the hierarchy.

Standard Buttons

Standard buttons are for primary actions. They have high emphasis and should be obvious.

Outlined Buttons

Outlined buttons are for secondary actions. They have medium emphasis - they are clear but not prominent.

Link Buttons

Link buttons are for tertiary actions. They have low emphasis - they are discoverable but unobtrusive.

Rounded and Squared Buttons

All buttons can be rounded or squared.

Button Sizes

Use the same -xs -sm -md -lg -xl modifier classes to change the size of your buttons.
